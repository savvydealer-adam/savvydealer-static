If you're running Google Vehicle Ads (VLAs) through Performance Max, a chunk of your spend is likely paying for clicks that don't move metal. Here's why it happens - and how our Free Waste Audit shows you exactly where the leaks are.

## What's Actually Happening

Vehicle Ads are great at putting real cars in front of real shoppers. But because they run through a "do-everything" campaign type, your dollars can drift-fast. The platform chases reach and "engagement," even when that engagement isn't a person ready to buy your car.

We see the same five budget leaks again and again:

## Leak #1: Low-Intent Clicks Steal Your Best Dollars

**What you notice:** Impressions and clicks look healthy, but showroom activity and solds don't.

**Why it wastes money:** Not all placements are equal. Some are perfect for shoppers; others are just eyeballs. Your budget doesn't always know the difference on its own.

**What we do:** Our audit shows where spend is flowing into soft traffic-so you can redirect it to higher-intent moments.

## Leak #2: Brand Traffic Hides the Real Story

**What you notice:** "Great results" when people search your dealership name - but prospecting looks weak.

**Why it wastes money:** Mixing brand with non-brand hides whether you're actually winning new shoppers.

**What we do:** We separate the signals and show what performance really looks like without the brand halo.

## Leak #3: Inventory Feed Friction

**What you notice:** Lots of views on the wrong cars, outdated prices in screenshots, or VDP clicks that don't line up.

**Why it wastes money:** Invisible feed issues (images, availability, links) create noise. Noise burns budget.

**What we do:** We grade your feed quality and show exactly which parts are costing you opportunities.

## Leak #4: Geo Sprawl Outside Your Trade Area

**What you notice:** Spend bleeding into "why are we advertising there?" zip codes.

**Why it wastes money:** Dollars aimed outside your core market rarely return.

**What we do:** We map where your money actually lands and quantify how much could be pulled back to high-yield areas.

## Leak #5: The Attribution Mirage

**What you notice:** "Leads" up, sales flat.

**Why it wastes money:** Counting easy conversions makes results look better than they are. If the platform doesn't see the right outcomes, it optimizes to the wrong ones.

**What we do:** We align measurement with what matters - so optimizations point to sold units, not vanity.

## Why This Keeps Happening (Even With Smart Teams)

- The campaign type is built to explore.
- Tools surface the "good news" by default.
- Feed and measurement issues are hard to spot from the front end.
- Everyone's busy: sales meetings, OEM programs, inventory turns-deep audits slide.

**Bottom line:** None of this means Vehicle Ads don't work. It means un-managed drift is expensive.
