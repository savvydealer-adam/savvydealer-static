When your site loads fast, more shoppers become leads. That's it. The rest - SEO wins, lower ad waste, and better placement in new AI-style search - flows from that one fact.

## What Speed Does for Sales (in Plain Numbers)

Pages that load in about 1 second convert ~3× better than those that take 5 seconds. That's from a large multi-year study tracking millions of visits.

Even tiny gains matter: cutting just 0.1 second of mobile load time lifted conversions 8-10% in Deloitte's cross-industry research.

**Takeaway:** If your SRPs/VDPs feel instant, more visitors start and finish forms - without buying a single extra click.

## Why Speed Helps You Rank (and Show Up in AI Results)

Google looks at real-world page experience. In simple terms, aim for:

- **Main content shows within ~2.5 seconds**
- **Clicks and taps get a visible response in under 0.2 seconds**
- **No jumping layout while the page loads**

These are the practical targets Google recommends for a great experience, and they're used by Google's ranking systems alongside relevance. Hitting them can tip the scales when your content is as helpful as the next dealer's.

AI features in Google Search (like AI Overviews) build on the normal index. There's no special "AI markup" - you win by being easy to crawl, fast, and clearly structured.

One more crucial point: **Google crawls less when your site responds slowly or throws errors**, which delays updates to your inventory in Search. Faster, steadier servers get crawled more.

## Where Dealer Sites Usually Slow Down

1. **Huge photos/videos on VDPs** without proper compression or responsive sizes.

2. **Too many third-party tags** (chat, widgets, trackers) loading before a shopper can do anything.

3. **Heavy JavaScript on inventory pages** that could be delivered as simple HTML first.

Fixing these three often produces the biggest lift in "it just feels instant."

## A Simple Checklist Your GM Will Appreciate

**Make the first view fast:** compress the hero image, send the right size for each device, and avoid anything that blocks the page from showing.

**Keep interactions snappy:** defer non-essential scripts so filters, menus, and forms react in under 0.2s.

**Stop layout "jumps":** reserve space for images/chat so buttons don't move under someone's finger.

**Help Google find cars fast:** clean sitemaps, reliable hosting, and (where relevant) vehicle listing structured data to surface inventory across Google surfaces.
