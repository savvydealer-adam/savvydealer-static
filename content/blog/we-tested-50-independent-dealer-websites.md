## The Performance Crisis Nobody's Talking About

We recently analyzed 50 dealership websites from the five most popular independent dealer website providers. These platforms serve thousands of dealers across North America and represent the backbone of how independent dealers compete online.

The performance data revealed a troubling pattern:

- **Less than 50% passed Google's Core Web Vitals assessment.**
- The average performance score was just **49 out of 100**. The highest score we found across all 50 sites was only 68—still failing by Google's standards.
- **Not a single site scored 80 or higher.**

This isn't about one bad provider or a few outlier websites. This is a systemic problem across the independent dealer website industry.

## Why Performance Scores Matter to Your Bottom Line

Most dealers don't think about performance scores until they understand what they actually measure: **how fast your website loads and responds to shoppers**.

Performance scores in the 40s and 50s translate to painfully slow load times. When a customer is comparison shopping between you and three other dealers, your site needs to load instantly. If it doesn't, they've already moved on to your competitor before your first vehicle photo even appears.

**Core Web Vitals measure three critical factors:**

1. **Largest Contentful Paint (LCP)**: How quickly your main content loads
2. **First Input Delay (FID)**: How fast your site responds to clicks and taps
3. **Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS)**: Whether your page jumps around while loading

Google uses these metrics as ranking factors. Failing sites get pushed down in search results. When shoppers search for "used cars near me" or "buy here pay here [city]," they see your competitors with faster sites instead.

But the bigger cost isn't SEO—it's the customers who leave before your page even loads.

## The Root of the Problem

Most independent dealer platforms were built during a different era of automotive retail. They were designed when:

- Desktop browsing was still dominant
- Page speed wasn't a Google ranking factor
- AI search engines didn't exist
- "Having a website" was enough to compete

The platforms haven't evolved. They're still running on the same template-driven, shared-hosting architecture they launched with years ago. Speed and performance weren't design priorities then, and retrofitting them now would require rebuilding from scratch.

Meanwhile, Google's standards keep rising. Core Web Vitals became a ranking factor in 2021. AI search engines like ChatGPT and Perplexity are changing how shoppers research cars. Mobile-first indexing means your mobile performance is your ranking.

Independent dealers are being left behind by their own platforms.
